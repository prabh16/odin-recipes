Channa Masala

Description

Channa Masala is a hearty and flavorful North Indian dish made with chickpeas simmered in a spiced tomato-onion gravy. It's a popular vegetarian dish that's both filling and nutritious, commonly served with rice or flatbreads like roti, naan, or bhatura.

Packed with aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, garam masala, and turmeric, this dish delivers a bold and comforting flavor profile. It's perfect for cozy dinners or as part of a festive Indian meal, and it's easy to make with pantry staples.

Ingredients

  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon rice flour
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 (15 ounce) cans garbanzo beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  2. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chili. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil begins to separate from the masala.
  5. Mix in the turmeric, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for another minute.
  6. Add the chickpeas and stir to coat them with the masala.
  7. Pour in a little water to adjust the consistency and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  8. Sprinkle garam masala on top and give it a final stir.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice, roti, or naan.
